### Delphi-2M

- "primary prevention is a great and largely unfulfilled need, that in light of recent advances it should be given the highest priority. The key problem is that we haven’t had the ability to do it. _Until now._"
- Delphi-2M model: trained on UK biobank (400k participants) and validated on a Danish population of ~2M people. ![[Screenshot 2025-11-07 at 8.48.14 AM.png]]
- The model had an AUC of 0.76 over a 10-year span prediction, being just as good as single disease predictive models for dementia or heart disease![[Screenshot 2025-11-07 at 8.50.32 AM.png]]
- it was able to predict multiple diseases for an individual, tied to the timing of appearance of the disease. 
- the Delphi-2M model **does not** include things like polygenic risk score, genome sequence, biomarkers, unstructured EHR text, epigentic markers


### Personal Health Agent

- multi-agent system to act as a recommender system based on input from wearables, EHRs, lab reports -> I wonder how different this is from my WHOOP AI coach... ![[Screenshot 2025-11-07 at 9.01.06 AM.png]]
- Clinicians and health experts ranked the multi-agent system as the "most effective system for helping users achieve their goals in 80% of cases compared with 4% for a single-agent or 15% for a non-orchestrated multi-agent parallel system."


### Concluding
- current way of doing preventative medicine is through vaccinations, blood work (lipid panels, fasting glucose HbA1c, metabolic panel, CBC, electrolytes), and promoting healthy behaviors
- not even all doctors or patients abide by these
- in the future, there's the promise of using polygenic risk scores, models like Delphi-2M, continuous protein monitoring, epigenetic clocks, different classes of biomarkers, AI on labs, pathology, imaging, real time wearable data, digital twins
- We need to move towards primary prevention, rather than secondary prevention (which we already work on and are doing)
