**Situation** Biotech’s growth depends on extracting reliable insights from increasingly complex datasets. Computational scientists and ML engineers are core to evidence quality across discovery and development.

**Complication** Today, computational biologists risk being treated as service providers rather than scientific co-owners, while ML/infra specialists risk invisibility when tools are not adopted. Without clear governance and equal career ladders, prestige and retention will suffer.

**Question** How should we structure roles, decision rights, and advancement criteria so that both embedded (program-facing) and central (infra-building) staff have equal influence and growth opportunities?

**Answer** We will establish a **dual-track structure with shared governance**:

## **Governance Model**
- **Reporting line:** Department reports to CSO (default) or CTO (if modality requires).
- **Decision rights:**
	- *Embedded computational biologists* are accountable for program-level analyses at stage-gates.
	- *Central ML/infra specialists* are accountable for shared pipelines, reproducibility, and build/buy decisions.
- **Rituals:** Weekly “Data Sciences Guild” for cross-sharing; quarterly joint review with CSO/CTO.

## **Career Ladders**
**Program/Scientific Track (Computational Biologists)**
- Associate → Scientist → Senior Scientist → Principal Scientist → Distinguished Computational Biologist
- Criteria: Stage-gate impact, biological translation, cross-functional credibility.
**Technical/Infrastructure Track (ML & Infra)**
- Engineer → Senior Engineer → Staff Engineer → Principal Engineer → Distinguished ML Architect
- Criteria: Platform adoption, reproducibility, regulatory readiness, cross-program leverage.
**Shared Milestones**
- Senior/Principal levels eligible for **Departmental Leadership Council** (budget, hiring, strategy influence).
- Promotion criteria published and calibrated across both tracks.

## **Success Metrics**
- **Embedded:** % programs with computational co-leads; program satisfaction.
- **Central:** % programs using standard pipelines; infra adoption within 2 quarters.
- **Org-wide:** DS retention, cross-track promotions, contributions to IP/regulatory filings.
