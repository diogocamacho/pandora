**Exercise: Teach your GPT **

On this fun exercise, something that was highlighted in the WSJ article and that @Vini Maniasked me: how do we make the GPT act more like each one of us? Meaning: how can the answers be more like you, from text to everything else. It just so happens that the first 

Step is relatively simple, and it all as to do with setting your settings right. When you log in to your Enterprise GPT, look at the little Flaghship logo in the top right hand corner. When you click on it you will see an option that says: “Customize ChatGPT”. When you navigate to it, you will see a little window pop up. This is where you can customize it. You can have it call you “Master” or “Obi-wan”, you can tell it what you do, but, _**more importantly**_ you can configure its traits and what it needs to know about you. Here is where you can be detailed about how it should interact with you (traits) and what it should know about you. As an example, the traits that I have defined on mine are:  Take a forward-thinking view. 

Concise. 

Challenges the status-quo. 

Future backwards perspective.

Pragmatic.

Takes no bullshit

goes deep into topics and brings up the most important points.

 

And do keep in mind that these are not static. If you want to refine how it answers you, edit these. Be explicit. Your experience will benefit. So, @Michael Hamill, maybe the different voice it’s using with you when you ask it questions can also be tailored… 😉

 

 

**Prompt of the week**

For today/s prompt, something simple related to some work we are doing at FL110:  “What amino acids are more easily chemically modified to allow for protein identification? Please be descriptive and support your answer on hard data. Provide me a table of likelihood of modification and abundance in protein for each amino acid.”

 

While this is not the best prompt that you can write, a couple of things to note:

* Notice how I’.  being prescriptive about what I want the model to look for (hard evidence)
* Notice what I’m asking for the model to give me a table as output. Though I didn’t specify it, I could ask the model to turn that table into an excel file to load into my workflow later on.

 

Why is this critical? There was a fun article on Medium a few months ago ([here](https://medium.com/aimonks/why-chatgpt-is-the-new-google-445d3de5ca2f)) which kinda hints that you can, effectively, turn ChatGPT into your Google searches.
