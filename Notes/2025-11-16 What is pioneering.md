# What is pioneering?

Pioneering has many definitions. The one we like at Flagship comes from ecology: a pioneering species is the first to enter a barren environment—where no life exists—and settle in, making it possible for other forms of life to follow. For instance, it’s the first thing that grows after a volcanic eruption. Pioneering is about **venturing into a place where there are no clear signs of how to live, how to protect yourself, or even where to begin, yet finding a way to survive, organize, and ultimately thrive**.

Think of it in simple terms, like deciding to go camping: 
- One approach is to plan a trip to an established campsite, where you can check for electricity, water, and the presence of other people. 
- The other is to just get in your car, drive into the middle of the woods, and decide, _“I’m going to camp here.”_ In that case, there’s nothing ready for you, and you may not even know if it’s safe. 

The mindset is different, and so are the fears you face. But those fears are part of the choice you made to go there.

**It's a willful choice to start from scratch**: A truly pioneering idea rarely fits neatly into existing systems. When something entirely new enters a world built for what already exists, you may need to redesign the whole value chain to support it.
