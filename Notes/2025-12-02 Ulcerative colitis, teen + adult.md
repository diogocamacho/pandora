### Problem Framing

1. Microbial energy deficit: reduced SCFAs (butyrate/propionate) with excess sulfide and lactate suggests impaired colonocyte fuel and barrier function
2. Bile acid dysmetabolism: decreased secondary with increased primary conjugates, implying altered FXR/TGR5 signaling and pro-inflammatory bile acid pool
3. Tryptophan axis skewed toward kynurenine with loss of microbial indoles, consistent with heightened IDO activity and reduced AHR ligands
4. Pro-inflammatory eicosanoid tone elevated (e.g., PGE2), amplifying mucosal inflammation

### Formulation Scientist Rationale

An enteric capsule combining microencapsulated sodium butyrate with resistant starch was chosen to restore SCFA-driven colonocyte energy and barrier integrity directly in the distal colon, delivering broad mechanism coverage with a food-derived safety profile and good GI tolerance. We accept divided dosing across multiple capsules and some manufacturing complexity to mask butyrate odor and ensure precise colonic release, avoiding powders or softgels that compromise palatability or targeting.

Strategy angle: colon-targeted SCFA restoration, capsule-based
