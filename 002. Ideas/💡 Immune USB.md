## What if…
What if we could build a universal immune interface — a single engineered receptor expressed in immune cells that acts like a biological USB port, allowing plug-in adaptor molecules to reprogram targeting and function in real time? This would transform T cells, NK cells, or macrophages into living, modular devices: same chassis, infinitely adaptable targeting through simple molecular swaps.

## 🧬 Scientific Rationale
Immune recognition is encoded by receptor-ligand pairs — rigid systems that define specificity and signaling strength. By decoupling recognition (extracellular binding) from activation (intracellular signaling), we gain full programmability.

Existing modular receptor systems (SUPRA CARs, ConvertibleCARs, UniCARs) have validated the feasibility of adaptor-controlled immunity but remain single-pathway and lack dynamic control. Synthetic biology and protein design now make it possible to create orthogonal binding interfaces, tune receptor clustering thresholds, and integrate logic control through synthetic transcription factors (e.g., SynNotch, NFAT-fusion).

The scientific frontier is combining modular architecture + dynamic adaptors + control logic into a single framework. This would enable reversible, multiplex immune programming — a biological control layer between antigen and action.

With AI-guided protein design (for orthogonal receptor–adaptor pairs), in silico docking, and real-time cytokine sensing loops, the Universal Immune Switch could provide a plug-and-play immune “OS” capable of handling multiple diseases, switching targets safely, and scaling across cell types.

## 💼 Business Rationale
Current cell therapies are bespoke — each CAR or TCR program is a new company, new trial, new cost center. This platform converts the model from asset-centric to platform-licensable: one universal receptor, many plug-in adaptors.

- Platform scalability: Each new disease becomes a software-like update, not a new drug.
- Economic efficiency: 80% of development (cell line, manufacturing, safety validation) is reused.
- Partnerability: Pharma can license disease-specific adaptors while the core receptor IP remains proprietary.
- Regulatory advantage: Shared safety and pharmacology data across adaptors accelerate filings.

## ⚡ Why Now
1. The convergence of programmable biology and AI-designed proteins has made it feasible to design orthogonal receptor–ligand pairs that don’t cross-react with native human pathways. This removes one of the biggest historical bottlenecks for modular immune systems.
2. Next-generation cell chassis (from universal donor CAR-T/NK programs to mRNA-encoded immune cells) are now clinically validated, providing plug-and-play backbones for modular designs.
3. The regulatory pathway for adaptor-controlled immunotherapies is opening — the FDA has accepted modular INDs (e.g., for SUPRA CAR, UniCAR), acknowledging the “shared core” model across programs.
4. Manufacturing scalability has shifted: GMP lines can now produce both engineered cells and recombinant adaptor proteins in the same facility type, closing the loop on “immune software updates.”
5. Demand signal: post-CAR-T toxicity and narrow targeting have created strong commercial and clinical hunger for safer, reversible, and retargetable immune therapies.  

In short: we can finally separate the “immune engine” from the “immune software” — and the tools to safely do it exist right now.
