
Shirt brands for me to try:
**British makers:**

- **Turnbull & Asser** (~$300-400) – Jermyn Street legend, bespoke-quality ready-to-wear
- **Emma Willis** (~$300-500) – exceptional British craftsmanship, beautiful fabrics
- **Harvie & Hudson** (~$150-250) – solid Jermyn Street option, less flashy than T&A

**Italian (often better value than British):**

- **Finamore** (~$200-300) – Neapolitan, beautiful soft collars, excellent hand feel
- **Luca Faloni** (~$180-250) – modern cuts, Italian fabrics, great online experience
- **Barba Napoli** (~$250-350) – if you want the absolute best Italian shirting

**European alternatives:**

- **Eton** (your instinct is right, ~$200-300)
- **Stenströms** (~$150-250) – Swedish, similar quality to Eton, slightly better value
- **Van Laack** (~$200-300) – German precision, excellent fabrics

**Best value in premium:**

- **Ledbury** (~$150-200) – American brand, Italian fabrics, excellent construction
- **Proper Cloth** (~$100-200) – made-to-measure, customize everything, incredible fit
- **Kamakura** (~$80-120) – Japanese precision, exceptional value, comparable to brands 2x the price



**Blazers:**

**Fit:**

- **Modern tailored fit** – not slim (too trendy/restrictive) or classic (too boxy). You want structured shoulders, clean through the chest, slight taper at waist
- **Unstructured or half-lined** – more comfortable, contemporary, works in biotech's business-casual environment
- **Two-button, notch lapel** – timeless and versatile
- Length should cover your seat, sleeves should show about 1/4-1/2 inch of shirt cuff

**Brands (by tier):**

_Investment pieces ($800-1500):_

- **Suitsupply** – excellent quality-to-price ratio, modern cuts, half-canvas construction
- **Spier & Mackay** – fantastic value, true half-canvas, contemporary fit
- **Brooks Brothers (Milano fit)** – classic American, recently improved their modern cuts

_Premium ($1500-3000):_

- **Canali** – Italian craftsmanship, beautiful fabrics, exec-appropriate
- **Hickey Freeman** – American-made, impeccable construction
- **Ermenegildo Zegna** – if you want to signal serious success

_Approachable luxury ($500-800):_

- **Bonobos** – great fit options, easy returns, good for dialing in your size
- **J.Crew Ludlow** – solid construction, modern cut, accessible


**Pants:**

**Fit:**

- **Flat front, no pleats** – cleaner, more modern
- **Mid-rise** – sits at natural waist (where you're wearing them now)
- **Slim straight or straight leg** – not skinny, not baggy. Clean line from hip to ankle
- **Slight break or no break** – contemporary, shows your shoes

**Fabrics:**

- Wool or wool-blend for year-round versatility
- Cotton chinos for warmer months/casual days
- Avoid overly casual fabrics in meetings

**Brands:**

_Everyday workhorses ($100-200):_

- **Bonobos** – excellent fit options (they have multiple rises and cuts), easy online ordering
- **Banana Republic** – reliable, good sales, Fulton or Aiden cuts
- **J.Crew** – solid quality, various fits

_Step up ($200-400):_

- **Incotex** – Italian-made, beautiful drape, executive-quality
- **PT01/PT05** – contemporary fit, premium fabrics
- **Brooks Brothers** – Clark or Milano fits

**Colors to own:**

1. Navy (you've got this)
2. Charcoal gray
3. Medium gray
4. Olive or forest green (versatile fourth option)


**Shoes:**

**Styles you need:**

_The essentials:_

1. **Brown leather cap-toe oxfords** – most versatile, works with everything
2. **Dark brown leather loafers** (penny or tassel) – slightly less formal, very exec
3. **Burgundy/oxblood leather derbies or brogues** – adds color, still professional

_Nice to have:_ 4. **Suede loafers** (brown or navy) – texture variation for less formal days 5. **Chelsea boots** (brown leather) – modern, works for campus casual or travel

**Brands (by tier):**

_Entry luxury ($300-500):_

- **Allen Edmonds** – American classic, excellent quality, can be resoled. Park Avenue (oxfords) or Strand (brogues)
- **Meermin** – Spanish-made, outstanding value, Goodyear welted
- **Beckett Simonon** – made-to-order, great quality-to-price

_Investment ($500-800):_

- **Alden** – American icon, bulletproof construction, timeless
- **Carmina** – Spanish craftsmanship, beautiful lasts
- **Crockett & Jones** – British heritage, impeccable quality

_Premium ($800+):_

- **John Lobb** – if you want the absolute best
- **Edward Green** – exquisite, heirloom quality
- **Gaziano & Girling** – bespoke-level ready-to-wear

**Real advice for your role:**

Start with **Allen Edmonds Park Avenue in walnut brown** and **a pair of quality brown leather loafers**. These two will cover 80% of your needs. Then add burgundy oxfords or suede loafers as your third pair.
