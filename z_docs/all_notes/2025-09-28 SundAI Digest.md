**Prompt of the week**

On to digital twins. I won’t be super extensive in the interest of keeping this short, but I was thinking about how we can use AI to learn more about how we think and the things we are interested in outside of the settings for ChatGPT. Can we create our own digital twin, that is aware of our writing style, out interests, what we have done, and so on. This is challenging, as we are a mish mosh of life experiences that an AI cannot capture, but if it can help in tasks like email editing/replying, or writing reports or being a thought partner and/or advisor, it’s a step in the right direction. 



So this is what I’ll challenge you with: Open ChatGPT and under GPTs create a new one. To keep it simple, use the “Create” feature (instead of the “Configure” one) to have a chat with the GPT as it builds your digital self. Something like this:

“I want to create a GPT that is my Digital Twin. I want it to think like me, write like me, reply to me as if I’m having a conversation with myself, be a thought partner, be an advisor, be an innovator.”



Then follow its prompts and questions. Let’s see how far you get. The one thing that you should have on hand is the knowledge you want it to have. For example, maybe you want it to know what you have done for your career (give it your CV), you want it to learn your academic constributions (give it your papers or google scholar profile), you want it to learn your email writing style in different contexts (replying to an executive, a KOL, a board member, a colleague), what interests you have (if you want to keep honing in those skills in your guitar playing to master that tricky Megadeth solo, or when to best plant tomatoes and peppers in New England). I am curious to see how far you get. Because the next level (using the Configure feature) will give you more control and flexibility but it’s a little more involved.
