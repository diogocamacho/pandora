# First month

1. Establishing identity
	1. Be visible
	2. work hard to allocate 5-10% to informal chats with team
	3. go see people where they work
	4. get to know people 
		1. voicemail
		2. email
		3. video calls
2. Clarifying performance expectations
	1. clarify boss’s expectations
		1. for you AND the team
	2. Discuss team goals
		1. goal setting is a collaborative dialogue
		2. don’t mandate
		3. follow up with personal 1:1 chats
		4. define team norms
			1. informal rules of behavior that guide the group
			2. ideas:
				1. positive,
				2. respectful,
				3. timely
			3. should have 5 ideas of so
				1. distirbute
				2. enforce
				3. practice what you preach
3. Communicating effectively
	1. when in doubt, reach out (dont micromanage but keep in loop)
	2. ask questions
		1. "what did you hear?”
	3. predict challenges before these arise
	4. candor vs civility
		1. candor is the most important
	5. communication channels
		1. the more important the message, the highest quality needed (live face to face)
	6. LISTEN
		1. listen more than you talk
		2. listening is giving team **voice**
		3. more listening —> more you learn about team
4. Embracing feedback
	1. delivering feedback
		1. be specific! NOT general or vague! say exactly what you mean
		2. deliver feedback positively, even if difficult
		3. consider how much to give
			1. give what you feel they can take
				1. too little: waste of time
				2. too much: offend
		4. use “I statements”
			1. “I rated you a 3 because…"
			2. “I made this decision because…”
5. A focus on purpose
	1. What is your _purpose_? 
		1. work without purpose is just a paycheck
	2. where does purpose come from?
		1. fit: job + personality + skills
		2. finding fit can be difficult
	3. build positive relationships
		1. no jerks allowed
	4. recognition and rewards!
	5. connect team to outcomes

# Connecting with the team

1. Building initial rapport
	1. personal conversations (be a person, not just a professional)
	2. work hard to _see_ the _individual_. you will see people, not just employees
	3. show gratitude and respect
	4. encourage debate
2. Explaining decsion-making style
	1. forms:
		1. autocratic: you make decision with no input and tell decision
		2. collaborative: you make decions, you seek genuine input —> preferred
		3. democrative: team makes decision independent of your input
	2. explain all decisions
		1. good explanations make decisions more just
	3. OWN YOUR DECISIONS
3. From team member to team leader
	1. managing friends and colleagues
		1. address issue early and openly
		2. “door is open, talks are confidential"
		3. tell them what you _no longer do_ (adds clarity)
		4. (check “managing employee performance problems)
	2. informal 1:1s
	3. decision resolve
		1. if you change a decision to appease others, you’re in trouble

# Building trust

1. signaling fairness and integrity
	1. fair: create a positive, transparent workplace
		1. “treat people the same by creating positive and transparent work place, but also treat them differently based on their performance and their needs.”
	2. integrity and fairness matter! 
	3. trust takes a long time to build but can be lost in an instant
	4. ACTIONS speak louder than WORDS
	5. share credit widely
	6. share the pain
	7. be willing to get your hands dirty!! 
2. Becoming a servant leader
	1. need to build you employees as FIRST priority
	2. commitme to employee development
		1. seek to support professional and personal growth
	3. starts with self awareness
		1. strengths
		2. weaknesses
		3. personality
	4. Listening
		1. servant leader listens more than speaks
	5. empathy
	6. ability to facilitate healing
	7. "servant leadership is practical”
3. Increase your authenticity
	1. {being authentic means to be open, to show humanity, to be humble, and always model the way}
	2. these make your confidence and competence shine
	3. be decisive but also inclusive
	4. be humble
		1. listening more than speaking
		2. share authority even when you don’t need to 
		3. commend team 
	5. walk the talk

# Using authority successfully

1. Asserting authority
	1. __authority__: right of a person to exercise influence and make a decision
	2. start small
	3. turn a detractor into a supporter
	4. give more autonomy (no micro management)
	5. right to act, but not alwasy easy to use
2. Looking back to move forward
	1. team culture is evolution over time
	2. complete the legwork to understand where they've been
	3. don’t rush into action just to make your mark
	4. team evolved because of team members, past and current
	5. the better you understand the past, the quicker you can move forward
3. developing a lieutenant
	1. can step in when you can’t
	2. great at initiating followers (catalyst to support)
	3. conduit for feedback on the team (unfiltered feedback to you)
	4. serve as personal advocate
	5. can be a big part of your personal succession plan
	6. they are NOT a clone of you
	7. they are NOT a “yes man”
	8. they are NOT an enforcer

# Managing change

1. Diversity and inclusion
	1. DEI == diversity of ideas
	2. groups are first awkward in the path to becoming awesome
	3. leaders modeling the way
2. A process perspective
	1. assess capacity for change
		1. orgs change too much
	2. build the case for change
	3. write the script
		1. team to guide the effort, communication team, rollout
	4. talk about failure
	5. co-opt a few people who may resist the change (pose a challenge)
	6. activate the communications plan
	7. provide training related activities
	8. rollout
	9. face resistance:
		1. help resistors embrace challenge
3. Change starts with you: model the way
	1. “be the change you want in the world”
	2. lead by example
	3. talk the talk AND walk the walk
