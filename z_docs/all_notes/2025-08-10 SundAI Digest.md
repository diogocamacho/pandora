**What’s the fuss with ChatGPT 5.0?**

The claim with ChatGPT 5.0 is that this model has “PhD level intelligence”. The model itself (outside from that silly claim), has a larger context window (doubling the 128k token window to a 256k token window — recall from last week what that means: it remembers more). The reviews are mixed, with some users seemingly loving it, and others abhorring it (there doesn’t seem to be much of a gray zone. I don’t know when this model will be available for Enterprise GPT users (usually there’s a lag between releasing a model and us having access to it by a couple of weeks or so), but I had a go at it with my free personal account. I tried it with a few silly things (not worth mentioning), but then I asked it to be honest and give me a comparison between 4o and itself. 



Yes, of course it’s touting itself, and we need a comprehensive assessment with real examples (I’ll defer to the Digital team), but it’s good to have this as a basis for comparison.

I’m not a user of NotebookLM, but Brad is. So, I asked Brad to provide me a few sentences on how he uses it:



This is as much as a user feedback as you’d like to get. So I decided to give it a go, on a topic that I would be interested in and somewhat familiar with. I gave the model 29 papers and waited for it to process them all, and then started interfacing with the model to generate mind maps and evolve ideas around those.
