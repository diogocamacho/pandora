The KPMP atlas exists [here]([https://atlas.kpmp.org](https://atlas.kpmp.org/)). It is an atlas specific for kidney that has a lot of data on diseases and healthy samples. The idea behind what we are trying to do is to use this data set to define addresses that are specific for kidney cells, to use as potential targets in Abiologics target ID efforts.

A description of the data itself can be found [here](https://www.kpmp.org/help-docs/study-overview). A ChatGPT summary of the data:

### 1. Study design & cohorts

- KPMP is a prospective cohort study whose goal is to integrate **deep molecular phenotypes of kidney biopsies** with **longitudinal clinical phenotypes** in acute kidney injury (AKI) and chronic kidney disease (CKD).

- Participant populations include:
    - CKD: especially in the context of diabetes (DKD – diabetic kidney disease) or hypertension‐associated CKD.
    - AKI: focusing on intrinsic non‐glomerular disease (especially acute tubular injury/necrosis)
    - A “resilient” diabetes population (DM-R): individuals with long‐standing diabetes (> 25 years) **without** clinically‐evident kidney disease. This is interesting because it offers a protective‐phenotype angle.

- Enrollment categories (before biopsy) vs primary adjudicated categories (after review of histopathology + clinical data) are defined. For example: Healthy reference, CKD, AKI, DM-R.

- The adjudication committee uses whole‐slide images, clinical packet (history, labs, meds), pathology report including EM images to define primary and secondary disease categories. 

---

### 2. Biospecimens & tissues

- Each participant undergoes a kidney biopsy. The aim is 3 cores per participant:
    - Core 1: diagnostic purposes.
    - Core 2 and Core 3: research purposes (for multi‐omics, imaging, etc).

- In addition to tissue from biopsy, biospecimens: urine, plasma, serum, DNA, stool (baseline and longitudinal) are collected.

- Demographic, clinical, laboratory data also collected longitudinally. (Baseline at time of biopsy + follow‐up)

---

### 3. Clinical phenotyping data

- A “baseline clinical phenotyping data file” exists: sex, age, race, disease type, KDIGO stage (kidney disease staging), duration of disease, comorbidities, personal & family history, lab values, medications.
- There is also longitudinal follow‐up phenotype data (so not just snapshot).
- Clinical data uses the REDCap codebook for standardization.

---

### 4. Pathology data

- From biopsy tissue: processed for light microscopy (LM), immunofluorescence (IF), and electron microscopy (EM).
- LM stains used: H&E, PAS, trichrome, Jones’ silver.
- Whole‐slide images are available for download in the Atlas Data Repository.

---

### 5. Molecular (multi‐omics) and imaging data

- A suite of approved “tissue interrogation technologies” (i.e., multi‐omics + imaging). The list (with some “pending approval” items) provides a snapshot of what has been or will be collected:
    
    - **Transcriptomics**:
        - 10× Multiome: single-nucleus RNA-seq (snRNA-seq) + snATAC-seq (chromatin accessibility)
        - Single‐cell RNA-seq (scRNA-seq)
        - Spatial transcriptomics
    - **Proteomics**:
        - Multimodal Imaging Mass Spectrometry (pending approval)
    - **Metabolomics**:
        - Spatial metabolomics
    - **Epigenetics**:
        - Cut&Run
        - DNA methylation sequencing
    - **Imaging**:
        - 3D tissue imaging and cytometry
        - CODEX (multiplexed immunofluorescence)
        - Imaging Mass Cytometry
        - (Pending) Multiplexed Ion‐Beam Imaging
        - Spatial lipidomics
        - Spatial N-glycomics
- Metadata standards: all datasets must be accompanied by standard metadata templates (varying by technology) to enable interoperability.

---

### 6. Data access & standards

- Data is accessible via an “Atlas Data Repository” (with download capability for whole‐slide images etc) and via “Controlled access” files under a Data Use Agreement (DUA).
- Citation requirements are spelled out: The data is “results in whole or part based upon data generated by KPMP… Accessed Month Day, Year.”

---

### 7. Why this matters (and how this fits into your world)

- The project is trying to **re-classify kidney diseases** (new ontologies) by integrating high‐dimensional tissue/molecular data plus clinical trajectories. That aligns well with what you do (computational biology, multi‐omics integration).
- Because multiple modalities (sc/snRNA, ATAC, spatial, imaging, metabolomics etc) are collected from the _same core/participant_, this enables **multi‐modal integration** (which you focus on) — e.g., linking chromatin accessibility to transcript to spatial context.
- Longitudinal clinical + biospecimens allow linking “what we observe in the molecular tissue” to “how disease evolves clinically” — something that lends itself to machine learning, GNNs, differential modelling.
- The metadata standardisation is crucial for interoperability and downstream modelling (so your work can build on/plug into this ecosystem).

**Summary:**  
The KPMP Atlas integrates deep molecular data (transcriptomic, epigenomic, proteomic, metabolomic) with clinical, pathological, and imaging data from kidney biopsies and longitudinal phenotyping. The multi-modal design allows mapping of cell states, pathways, and spatial organization underlying CKD and AKI. Metadata adherence and open-access frameworks position the Atlas as a cornerstone for computational modeling, ML-driven phenotyping, and data harmonization in precision nephrology.
