Custom GPTs.

I’ve built a few of these custom GPTs over the past few months, some for my own use, some for sharing with others. I’ve shown you how we can use ChatGPT 5 to write a prompt for us to build a GPT with. We can also use GPT5 to define the norms (which will be part of the custom GPT’s knowledge) by which your custom GPT can abide. So what custom GPTs do we have (and, again, apologies if the links break — email me if you’d like access to any of these in particular if the link doesn’t work)? Here’s a curated list: 

* BioArchitect: inspired by a conversation with Tom. Takes an idea (even wonky) and explores the white space with you, from the What if… to a potential ProtoCo plan
* Pitch Evaluator: How can we make our decks better and the science meetings we have with Avak more productive? This is the genesis of this custom GPT. Give it a PDF/PPT and watch it rip it apart and give you constructive criticism so that you improve how to improve the deck
* Data Digester: Sure, you like to write your data digests. But what if a custom GPT could do it for you? Drop in a PPT/PDF in here and it will extract the most compelling story for you to highlight in your data digest, write it all up, and deliver you a Word document that will require minimal edits. 
* PILOTeer: something that I quickly put together during the last PILOT meeting, it will work a disease you may be interested in and score it based on the definitions set out byt the PILOT team



As you can see, there’s a ton of things that you can think of doing (and Charlotte had an idea that could be interesting to follow up on, if it’s differentiating enough from existing ones). I am all for generating these custom GPTs, even if only for personal use (I made the joke of building your own personal trainer GPT to Tom and Caro the other day). Once IT yells at us, we’ll tone it down, but until then, create away!
